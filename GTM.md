# GTM

The exact target user for SpendLens is probably:

- startup founders
- technical co-founders
- indie hackers
- engineering managers at small-to-mid startups

Especially companies between:

```txt id="m1q8x2"
5–80 employees
```

These are usually the teams experimenting heavily with AI tools without having a dedicated procurement or finance optimization workflow.

A lot of these teams slowly accumulate:

- ChatGPT
- Claude
- Cursor
- Copilot
- Gemini
- API bills
- random subscriptions

Then one day they realize:
“why are we spending this much on AI every month?”

That’s the exact moment this tool becomes useful.

---

Right before wanting this tool, users are probably:

- checking Stripe invoices
- looking at OpenAI bills
- discussing AI tooling internally
- searching for “Cursor vs Copilot”
- searching “best AI coding tool”
- trying to reduce startup burn
- complaining about SaaS costs on X/Twitter

Places where these users actually hang out:

- r/startups
- r/SideProject
- r/indiehackers
- Indie Hackers
- Hacker News
- X/Twitter startup circles
- small founder Discord servers
- AI tooling communities
- Cursor and Claude discussions online

---

First 100 users with $0 budget would probably come from:

- posting demo videos on X
- sharing screenshots of large savings audits
- posting in startup communities
- replying to founders complaining about AI costs
- Hacker News launch
- Product Hunt launch
- Reddit posts with real audit examples
- engineering Slack/Discord communities

I think the viral loop here is the shareable audit page.

If someone sees:

```txt id="p1q8x2"
“We reduced $1200/month in AI spend”
```

they will probably want to test their own stack too.

That’s why the audit results page was designed to be visually shareable.

---

The unfair distribution advantage for Credex is that they are already directly connected to the AI infrastructure/credits ecosystem.

Most “AI spend optimizer” tools would struggle to monetize because they only identify the problem.

Credex actually has a business solution:
discounted AI infrastructure credits.

So the audit becomes:

```txt id="w1q8x2"
free value tool
→ identifies overspend
→ converts high-savings users into leads
```

---

If this worked in week 1, realistic traction would probably look like:

- 300–1000 visitors
- 50–150 completed audits
- 10–30 captured leads
- maybe 3–5 genuinely high-value prospects

Honestly, I think the biggest challenge would not be getting initial curiosity clicks.

The hard part would be:

- recommendation accuracy
- trust
- making finance people believe the recommendations are legitimate

If the audit quality feels fake or overly aggressive, users would stop trusting it very quickly.
