# METRICS

I think the most important metric for SpendLens would be:

```txt id="m1q8x2"
completed audits with meaningful savings detected
```

Not just total visitors.

The whole point of the product is identifying real overspending opportunities and converting those into useful leads for Credex.

If people visit but never finish audits, or audits show no meaningful savings, the tool probably is not creating much value.

---

# North Star Metric

My North Star metric would probably be:

```txt id="p1q8x2"
high-savings audits generated per week
```

Meaning:

- audits completed
- with significant savings opportunities
- from legitimate teams/startups

This feels more meaningful than:

- DAU
- pageviews
- generic traffic

because this is not a social app people use daily.

---

# Input Metrics

Some metrics that probably drive the North Star:

## Audit Completion Rate

How many users who open the form actually finish the audit.

If this is low:

- form may be too long
- users may not trust the product
- onboarding may be weak

---

## Lead Capture Rate

How many completed audits turn into captured leads.

Especially important for:

```txt id="w1q8x2"
high-savings users
```

because those are likely the most valuable.

---

## Share Rate

How often users share audit result pages.

This matters because the product has a natural viral loop:

```txt id="r1q8x2"
interesting savings result
→ screenshot/share
→ new visitors
```

If nobody shares results, organic growth becomes much harder.

---

# What I’d Instrument First

First analytics I would probably add:

- audit started
- audit completed
- average detected savings
- share button clicks
- lead capture submissions
- Credex CTA clicks
- drop-off point in audit form

Mainly to understand:

```txt id="x1q8x2"
where users stop trusting or losing interest
```

---

# What Would Trigger A Pivot

A bad sign would probably be:

- lots of traffic
- decent audit completion
- but very low lead capture or consultation interest

That would probably mean:

- recommendations are not trusted
- savings feel unrealistic
- product does not create enough perceived value

Another bad signal would be if most audits consistently show:

```txt id="t1q8x2"
very low savings opportunities
```

because then the product becomes less compelling as a lead-generation tool.

I think recommendation quality and trust are probably the most important long-term factors for whether SpendLens actually works or not.
