# ECONOMICS

SpendLens is basically a lead-generation tool for Credex.

The idea is:

```txt id="m1q8x2"
free audit
→ identify overspend
→ convert high-savings users into Credex customers
```

So the economics mostly depend on:

- how many audits happen
- how many people leave emails
- how many high-savings teams convert

---

# Rough Funnel Assumption

Example monthly funnel:

- 5000 visitors
- 20% complete audit
- 1000 completed audits

Out of those:

- maybe 15% leave contact info
- around 150 leads

Out of those:

- maybe 15–20 book a Credex consultation

Then:

- maybe 3–5 become paying customers

These are rough assumptions obviously, but they feel realistic enough for a niche B2B tool.

---

# What A Converted Lead Could Be Worth

I do not know Credex’s real margins, so this is only approximate reasoning.

If a startup is spending:

```txt id="p1q8x2"
$1000–5000/month
```

on AI tooling/infrastructure, even a small percentage margin on discounted credits could make a customer fairly valuable.

Assuming:

- average customer value = $2000–$10000/year

even a few successful conversions per month could make the audit tool financially useful.

---

# CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)

The advantage of SpendLens is that it can probably acquire users fairly cheaply because:

- audit results are shareable
- AI cost optimization is already a trending topic
- screenshots naturally work on Twitter/X and Reddit
- founders already complain publicly about AI SaaS costs

So early-stage CAC could potentially stay very low if traffic is mostly:

- Product Hunt
- Hacker News
- Reddit
- Twitter/X
- SEO
- founder communities

Realistically:

```txt id="w1q8x2"
$0–$20 CAC
```

for early users seems possible if distribution is mostly organic.

---

# What Makes This Profitable

The tool probably becomes worth maintaining if:

- audits are cheap to generate
- lead capture rate stays decent
- a small percentage of high-savings users convert

Even:

```txt id="r1q8x2"
2–5 customers/month
```

could justify the tool if the average contract value is meaningful enough.

The important part is that the tool is not random content marketing.

It directly identifies:

```txt id="x1q8x2"
people already overspending on AI
```

which makes the leads much higher intent than generic startup traffic.

---

# What Would Need To Be True For $1M ARR

For SpendLens to help drive something like:

```txt id="t1q8x2"
$1M ARR
```

a few things would probably need to happen:

- audit quality becomes genuinely trusted
- audit sharing creates organic distribution
- AI pricing becomes even more fragmented/confusing
- Credex closes a meaningful percentage of high-savings leads
- the tool evolves beyond simple heuristics into stronger benchmarking and procurement workflows

I think trust would become the biggest factor.

If the recommendations feel fake or overly aggressive, finance people would stop taking the tool seriously very quickly.
